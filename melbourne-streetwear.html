01

Up There Store

Melbourne's most respected independent. Tucked down Flinders Lane since 2007, Up There stocks the hard-to-find stuff — Norse Projects, Engineered Garments, Kapital, exclusive New Balance collabs — and actually knows what they're selling. No hype, all substance.

↗ Website ⊕ 69 Flinders Lane, CBD
02

Culture Kings

Two levels of pure streetwear chaos on Russell Street with a second entrance on iconic Hosier Lane. In-store DJs, floor-to-ceiling drops, and everything from Supreme to Mitchell & Ness. Loud, proud, and unapologetically maximal — exactly how it should be.

↗ Website ⊕ 19–25 Russell St, CBD
03

Supply Store

Quietly elite. Founded in 2002 and finally bringing its Sydney cred to Melbourne in 2019, Supply stocks Stone Island, Noah, WTAPS, and Death Rites in a space that feels like a gallery more than a shop. For the discerning collector, not the hype-chaser.

↗ Website ⊕ 29A Sutherland St, CBD
04

HAVN

Gertrude Street institution. What started as a small Fitzroy boutique has become a gallery-like 200sqm space showcasing Lemaire, Mfpen, Orslow, and Andersen-Andersen. Function and aesthetics at every price point. Come for the clothes, stay for the architecture.

↗ Website ⊕ 200 Gertrude St, Fitzroy
05

The Sure Store

Melbourne's home of Good Stuff. Skateboard decks, deadstock sneakers, The Hundreds, Raised By Wolves, For The Homies — all crammed into a Little Lonsdale space that rewards the dig. One of those stores where you walk in for one thing and leave with four.

↗ Website ⊕ 273 Little Lonsdale St, CBD
06

Hemley Store

Family-owned and fiercely independent on Brunswick Street, Fitzroy. Hemley curates contemporary streetwear and lifestyle brands with a sharp editorial eye — think Afends, Nike SB, and cult Australian labels alongside global names. Neighbourhood energy, global taste.

↗ Website ⊕ 257–259 Brunswick St, Fitzroy
07

KICKSTW

Australia's premier hype sneaker marketplace, hiding in plain sight inside the Royal Arcade. Air Jordan, Yeezy, Bape, KAWS, Bearbrick, Travis Scott collabs — 100% authenticated. Started online in 2013, now the physical destination for Melbourne's sneakerheads.

↗ Website ⊕ Royal Arcade, Lvl 2, 314 Little Collins St
08

Hand 2 Hand

Gertrude Street's best-kept secret. Hand 2 Hand runs a tight, no-filler selection of Carrots, Dickies, Chrystie NYC, Bad Apples, and Most — concise but surgically curated. No filler, no hype tax. The kind of shop that makes you feel like you found something nobody else knows about yet.

↗ Website ⊕ 120 Gertrude St, Fitzroy
09

P.A.M.

Perks And Mini — Melbourne's most psychedelic export. The Level 3 Curtin House flagship is equal parts boutique and art installation, stocking the brand's own kaleidoscopic graphics-heavy gear alongside carefully selected international labels. A genuine Melbourne original with a global cult following.

↗ Website ⊕ Level 3, 252 Swanston St, CBD
10

Distal Phalanx

Hidden on the fifth floor of the historic Nicholas Building on Swanston Street. Dark, considered, and stubbornly uncommercial — Rick Owens, emerging local designers, jewellery, and art publications under one roof. The kind of place that reminds you why physical retail still matters.

↗ Website ⊕ Level 5, 37 Swanston St, CBD
